Pre-Attentive Processing

100ms is around how long it takes you to initiate eye movement (and hence, focus your attention). If you can detect something in under 100ms, it's under the threshold of focused attention. You can process the information before focusing your attention on it. This is called Pre-attentive processing

Why is this useful? If you're designing a list of information (e.g., a twitter feed or a table of data), you often want to call attention to certain values. Just dumping all the data in front of a user creates a bad experience. If you can direct their attention to relevant / important data, you can guide their attention towards the data you want to illustrate.

Can't Pre-attentive Process This

The first example worked because there was a single feature to process: filled-in square. We can pre-attentively process a single distinguishing feature, but not multiple features. To demonstrate, the following example contains multiple filled circles and a single filled square. We cannot pre-attentively process this because of the multiple distinguishing features (filled and shape).

Can you spot where the square is? If it appears in the spot your eyes are already focused on, there's a greater chance you'll see it. If it appears outside of where you eyes are focused on, chances are you'll miss it.
